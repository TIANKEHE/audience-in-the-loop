Audience in the Loop

Viewer Feedback-Driven Content Creation in Micro-drama Production on Social Media

Overview

RQ1

What emerging roles and production workflows have micro-drama creators adopted?

RQ2

How do creators collaborate with audiences to iteratively shape plots and characters?

28 Participants

Including screenwriters, directors, producers, and MCN operators with up to 9 years of experience.

Interviews

In-depth semi-structured interviews focusing on creation workflows and platform responses.

Thematic Coding

Inductive coding by three researchers to extract core variables and behavioral patterns.

Research Results

1. Reconfiguration of Creative Workflow

Traditional "linear progression" is replaced by a compressed, platform-driven cycle. Micro-dramas center on "strong hooks" rather than traditional character arcs. Blurred boundaries have led to creators taking on multiple roles simultaneously, while new roles like Traffic Operation Managers bridge the gap between content and algorithms.

2. Iterative Creation Driven by Feedback

Creators rely on both Quantitative Metrics (completion rates, view counts) and Textual Feedback (comments, memes). Feedback permeates the entire process: from early topic selection to mid-stage character adjustments (e.g., increasing a popular supporting actor's screen time).

Real-time Iteration Emotional Hooks Data-Responsive Writing

3. Pitfalls & Risks

Overreliance on feedback leads to creative homogenization and creator fatigue. Challenges include data authenticity (fake traffic), AI's inability to interpret complex irony, and the marginalization of "silent majority" voices.

Discussion & Implications

Narrative Interactivity

Micro-dramas are participatory cultural products reshaped through distributed interaction. Algorithms amplify audience signals to construct a dynamic narrative community.

Design Implications

We suggest tools to help screenwriters interpret signals while maintaining coherence, and mechanisms to protect minority opinions and diversity.
