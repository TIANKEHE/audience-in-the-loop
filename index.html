Audience in the Loop

Viewer Feedback-Driven Content Creation in Micro-drama Production

Gengchen Cao*¹ | Tianke He*² | Yixuan Liu³ | RAY LC³⁺
¹Tsinghua University    ²Sichuan University of Media and Communication    ³City University of Hong Kong

Abstract

The popularization of social media has led to increasing consumption of narrative content in byte-sized formats. Such micro-dramas contain fast-pace action and emotional cliffs, particularly attractive to emerging Chinese markets in platforms like Douyin and Kuaishou.

Through 28 semi-structured interviews, we found that the short-turn-around workflow leads to writers taking on multiple roles simultaneously, iteratively adapting to storylines in response to real-time audience feedback in the form of comments, reposts, and memes. This work reveals audience interaction as a new paradigm for collaborative creative processes on social media.

The Workflow

Writers adapt to fast-paced, audience-directed cycles, moving beyond traditional platform bias to real-time iteration.

New Narrative Styles

Identification of AI-generated and Audience-responsive micro-drama paradigms.

Research Questions

  • RQ1: How do micro-drama writers navigate the step-by-step processes of iterative content creation under platform affordances?
  • RQ2: In what ways does real-time audience feedback (comments, memes) reshape the narrative structure of digital dramas?
